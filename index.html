Sajek Tour

Sajek Valley is located in Bangladesh, specifically in the Rangamati Hill District. To be more precise, itâ€™s situated in the northern part of the Chittagong Hill Tracts, and it's quite close to the border with Mizoram, India.

Information

Sajek is often called the "Queen of Hills" or the "Roof of Rangamati," and for good reason. It's a valley set among rolling, verdant hills, with stunning natural beauty. The landscape is characterized by high mountains, deep forests, and sprawling grasslands. One of the most captivating aspects of Sajek is the experience of being among the clouds. Sometimes, you can literally walk through them, which creates a surreal and breathtaking atmosphere. The valley is also home to several indigenous ethnic groups, like the Chakma, Marma, and Lusai, and their unique cultures increase the charm of the place. The lifestyle of the local people is quite simple and traditional, which offers a fascinating contrast to city life.

My experience

I visited Sajek Valley about one year ago. It was during the winter season, around December. I found that time of year to be particularly pleasant because the weather was mild and the skies were clear, offering fantastic views.

I went there with a group of my close friends. There were about five of us in total. We wanted to experience this natural beauty together and create some lasting memories. Traveling with friends made the trip even more enjoyable and memorable.
